Sourdough Naan

Sourdough Naan

Description

Sourdough-leavened flatbread fried in cast iron pan. Can be made same-day or refrigerated for longer fermentation.

Source: Breadtopia

Ingredients

  • 275g all purpose flour
  • 200g sourdough starter
  • 125g milk (more or less, depending on yogurt style)
  • 75g yogurt
  • 5g salt
  • Flour for countertop
  • Oil or butter for rolling pin
  • Optional: Minced garlic or herbs; add to dough before rolling (Step 3), or combine with butter to brush breads once cooked

Steps

  1. Mix all of the ingredients in a bowl and then hand-knead for a few minutes. Add extra flour if the dough is too sticky or more milk if it's too dry.
  2. Cover the dough and let rise by 70-100%. This can take several days in the refrigerator or 4-8 hours at room temperature (or more), depending on the temperature and starter ripeness.
  3. Scrape the dough onto a floured countertop, de-gas and divide it in eight pieces. Add minced garlic and/or herbs if desired.
  4. Roll the eight pieces into balls, keeping track of order so first rolled is first fried. Wait at least 10 minutes for gluten to relax before rolling each ball flat.
  5. On stovetop, preheat cast iron pan (10 inch or larger) to "medium"; heat to be adjusted as needed.
  6. Lightly oil rolling pin; lightly flour countertop, if needed. Roll out first ball to just under 1/4 inch thickness.
  7. Transfer the dough to the hot frying pan and cook each side for 1-2 minutes. Flip when the top of the dough bubbles and the sides curl under. You're aiming for brown spots - adjust heat accordingly.
  8. Place cooked naans on a rack to cool. While frying, you should have time to roll out another dough.
  9. When all naans cooked, brush with melted butter if desired. Once cooled, cover or wrap to prevent drying out.

Naans may be frozen and reheated in toaster.
