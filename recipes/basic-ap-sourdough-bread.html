Basic All-Purpose Sourdough Bread

Description

A basic all-purpose flour sourdough bread recipe. Can be baked "artisan-style" or in loaf pan. Possible to bake same day, depending on timing and starter strength, or next day with overnight refrigeration.

Source: Breadtopia

Ingredients

  • 100 grams sourdough starter*
  • 400 grams all purpose flour
  • 300 grams water
  • 8 grams salt

*Starter to be built in step 1.

Steps

Dough Preparation

  1. Early in the day, build 100 grams of sourdough starter by feeding 30g starter with 35g flour and 35g water. Allow 3-6 hours to ripen; when at least double in size, it is ready to use.
  2. Mix all dough ingredients together in large bowl. Cover, let sit for 15 minutes, and then stretch and fold the dough to form a smooth ball.
  3. Cover the dough and let it rise (bulk ferment) until it has almost doubled in size. May take 4-8 hours, depending on temperature of kitchen.
  4. Flour the work surface and get the dough onto it. Shape the dough into preferred shape and leave seam-side down.
  5. Let the dough rest on its seam for about a minute. During this time, depending on baking method, either flour proofing vessel or prepare loaf pan with oil or parchment paper.
  6. Place the dough in proofing vessel seam-side up, or in loaf pan seam-side down.
  7. Cover and let dough rise for final proof.
    • For non-loaf pan with refrigeration stage: proof 30 minutes at room-temperature, then refrigerate 8-16 hours.
    • For same-day baking or loaf pan: proof 1-2 hours at room temperature. If refrigeration stage desired for loaf pan, may then be refrigerated 8-16 hours.

Baking instructions will differ based on chosen baking vessel.

Artisan-Style Baking Instructions

  1. Preheat oven and baking vessel at 500°F for 30 minutes.
  2. Flip dough out of proofing vessel onto sheet of parchment paper or base of hot baking vessel. Score, cover, and return vessel to oven.
  3. If baking vessel is ceramic cloche, bake at:
    1. 500°F for 20 minutes, lid on
    2. 450°F for 5 minutes, lid on
    3. 450°F for 5-10 minutes, lid off
  4. If baking vessel is cast iron, bake at:
    1. 500°F for 15 minutes lid on and then place a baking sheet under the cast iron on the same shelf
    2. 450°F for 10 minutes, lid on
    3. 450°F for 5-10 minutes, lid off
  5. When baking is complete, the bread should have an internal temperature of at least 205°F and it should sound hollow when you knock on the bottom of the loaf.
  6. Ideally, cool for 2 hours before slicing.

Loaf Pan Baking Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F for about 15 minutes.
  2. Brush the top of the dough with water and place it in the oven on the middle shelf.
  3. Bake for 50-60 minutes, turning the pan halfway if the bread is browning unevenly. To firm up the bottom and sides of the loaf, take it out of the loaf pan for the last 5-10 minutes.
  4. When baking is complete, the bread should have an internal temperature of at least 200°F.
  5. Brush the still-warm crust with butter or oil to keep it soft.
  6. Ideally, cool for 2 hours before slicing.
